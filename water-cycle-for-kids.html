Water Cycle Diagram for Kids: How Does Water Move?

What is the Water Cycle?

Have you ever noticed puddles disappearing on a sunny day? Or wondered where rain comes from? That's the water cycle at work!

The water cycle is nature's way of recycling water. Water doesn't disappear - it just goes on an amazing journey. It goes up into the sky, comes back down as rain, and then goes up again. This happens over and over, forever!

Why Does Water Keep Moving?

The sun is like a giant heater in the sky. It warms up water and makes it rise into the air. When it gets cold up high, the water comes back down. It's like a never-ending circle!

The 3 Main Steps of the Water Cycle

Step 1: Evaporation - Water Goes Up

What happens: The sun heats water in oceans, lakes, and rivers. The water turns into invisible steam called "water vapor" and floats up into the sky.

Think of it like: A puddle disappearing on a hot day! The water doesn't vanish - it turns into invisible vapor and rises up.

Real example: When you hang wet clothes outside, the sun dries them. The water evaporates into the air!

Step 2: Condensation - Clouds Form

What happens: When water vapor rises high in the sky, it gets very cold. The cold air turns the vapor back into tiny water droplets. Millions of these droplets stick together to make clouds.

Think of it like: When you breathe out on a cold day and see your breath - that's condensation! Or when a bathroom mirror gets foggy after a hot shower.

Real example: Clouds are just billions of tiny water droplets floating together in the sky.

Step 3: Precipitation - Rain Falls

What happens: Clouds get heavier and heavier as more water droplets join together. Finally, they become too heavy to float, and water falls back to Earth as rain, snow, or sleet.

Think of it like: A sponge soaking up water. When it's too full, water drips out. Clouds work the same way!

Real example: Rain fills up rivers, lakes, and oceans. Then the sun heats that water again, and the cycle repeats!

Where Does Water Go?

Water in the Ocean

Most of Earth's water is in the ocean! The sun heats ocean water every day, making it evaporate. That's where a lot of rain comes from.

Water in Rivers and Lakes

When rain falls, it fills up rivers and lakes. Water flows down rivers and eventually reaches the ocean. Along the way, the sun keeps evaporating water back into the sky.

Water We Use

The water you drink today might be the same water dinosaurs drank millions of years ago! Earth recycles all its water through the water cycle. Pretty cool, right?

Amazing Fact: The water on Earth has been going through the water cycle for billions of years. We're drinking the same water that was here when dinosaurs lived!

Cool Water Cycle Facts for Kids

  • The water cycle has been happening for billions of years
  • Water on Earth today is the same water dinosaurs drank
  • 97% of Earth's water is in the oceans
  • Only 3% is freshwater, and most of that is frozen in ice
  • A water drop can spend about 3,000 years in the ocean before evaporating
  • Clouds can weigh more than 1 million pounds!
  • The fastest raindrop falls at about 20 miles per hour

Water Cycle Activities for Kids

Activity 1: Make Your Own Water Cycle in a Bag

What you need:

  • A clear zip-lock plastic bag
  • Water with a few drops of blue food coloring
  • Tape
  • A sunny window

What to do:

  1. Pour a little colored water into the bag (about 1/4 full)
  2. Seal the bag tightly
  3. Tape it to a sunny window
  4. Wait a few hours and watch what happens!

What you'll see: Water will evaporate from the bottom, form droplets at the top (condensation), and eventually "rain" back down inside the bag. It's a mini water cycle!

Activity 2: Evaporation Race

What you need: Water and different surfaces (plate, paper, cloth)

What to do:

  1. Put the same amount of water on different surfaces
  2. Place them in the sun
  3. Check every 30 minutes to see which dries first

What you'll learn: Water evaporates at different speeds depending on the surface and temperature!

Activity 3: Cloud in a Jar

What you need: A glass jar, hot water, ice cubes, hairspray

What to do:

  1. Pour hot water into the jar (about 1 inch deep)
  2. Spray a little hairspray into the jar
  3. Quickly put ice cubes on top of the jar
  4. Watch a cloud form inside!

What you'll see: The hot water creates water vapor, and the cold ice makes it condense into a visible cloud!

Important Water Cycle Words

Evaporation: When water turns into invisible vapor and rises into the air

Condensation: When water vapor cools down and turns back into liquid water droplets

Precipitation: Water falling from clouds as rain, snow, or sleet

Water Vapor: Water in gas form - it's invisible and floats in the air

Collection: When water gathers in rivers, lakes, and oceans

You Now Know How Water Cycles!

Remember: Water goes UP (evaporation), forms CLOUDS (condensation), and falls DOWN (precipitation). Then it happens all over again!

Try the activities above this week and watch the water cycle happen right before your eyes!
